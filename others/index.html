Collective/administrative responsibilities

  • 2019-2021: Responsible for the purchase of two eye-tracking devices for an EEG platform at the Department of Experimental Psychology at Ghent University, and the MRI platform at Ghent University Hospital. Market research, budget calculation, ordering and organization of training provided by the company providing the eye trackers (SR-Research). Administrative and technical support following the purchase.
     
  • Since 2020: Member of the oculometry support group - Department of Experimental Psychology, Ghent University. (“Eye-tracking support group”) - Training and support for new users, setting up of various documentation (tutorials, scripts, etc.) - Referent “Oculometry-EEG integration” of the group.
     
  • Since 2018: Support for an EEG and oculometry platform at the Department of Experimental Psychology of the University of Ghent. Training and support for new users, writing and updating documentation.
     
  • 2012-Present: Drafting and obtaining ethical committee approval (projects using fMRI, EEG and behavior; at Toulouse Univ. (Comité de Protection des Personnes) and Ghent Univ.).
     
  • 2012-2014: Representative of non-statutory (doctoral and post-doctoral students) on the board of the CerCo laboratory. (Toulouse, France)  
  • 2009-2010: Student representative at the CogMaster pedagogical council (Paris, France).

 

Acacdemic and Scientific Activities & Service

  • Since 2017: Ad-hoc reviewer for: Plos Computational Biology; NeuroImage; Journal of Neurophysiology; European Journal of Neuroscience; Cognitive, Affective, and Behavioral Neuroscience; Frontiers in Human Neuroscience; Biological Psychology; and ANR projects (National Research Agency; public funding body).
     
  • 2020: Co-organization of a workshop on representations of space and serial order in working memory with Prof. Wim Gevers (Université Libre de Bruxelles) - annual conference of the Belgian Association for Psychological Sciences (BAPS) 2020 (postponed to 2021). “Representations in working memory: spatial and ordinal information” (https://www.baps2021.be/sp-senoussi-gevers.html)
     
  • 2019: Co-organization of a workshop on the psychophysiology of cognitive control with Prof. Wim Gevers (Université Libre de Bruxelles) and Prof. Tom Verguts (University of Ghent) - annual conference of the Belgian Association for Psychological Sciences (BAPS) 2019. “Psychophysiological contributions to cognitive control” (https://events.uliege.be/baps2019/programme/)
     
  • 2018-2019: Guest speaker for one-day training workshops on multivariate analyses (MVPA) methods in neuroimaging. Special focus on M / EEG. Theoretical presentation and practical workshop on data. Followed by doctoral students, post-docs and researchers.
